Mid-Autumn Festival Lotus Root Stir-Fry

A traditional dish to celebrate the harvest moon

Ingredients

  • 12 ounces lotus root
  • 8 wood ear mushrooms
  • 1/2 cup red bell pepper
  • 1/4 cup chicken stock
  • 2 teaspoons oyster sauce
  • 1/4-1/2 teaspoon salt (or to taste)
  • 1/4 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/8 teaspoon white pepper
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 6 slices ginger
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 2 scallions
  • 1 tablespoon Shaoxing wine
  • 2 teaspoons cornstarch

Instructions

  1. Prep the lotus root by peeling them, trimming the ends, and thinly slicing them, until you have about 340g.
  2. Bring a large wok or pot of water to a boil and blanch the lotus root, wood ears, and bell peppers for 45 seconds. Drain thoroughly and set aside.
  3. Combine the chicken stock, oyster sauce, salt, sugar, and white pepper in a small bowl to create the sauce mixture and set aside.
  4. Add the oil to the wok over medium heat along with the ginger. Cook for 30 seconds to a minute, and then add the garlic and scallions.
  5. Cook for another 20 seconds or so, and then add the blanched vegetables.
  6. Stir-fry the vegetables for 1 minute. Then add the Shaoxing wine around the perimeter of the wok, followed by the sauce mixture.
  7. Cook for 30 seconds, until the sauce is at a simmer. Stir your cornstarch and water mixture, making sure the cornstarch hasn't settled and add it to the wok.
  8. Stir-fry for another 20-30 seconds until the sauce coats the vegetables and serve.

Mid-Autumn Festival Significance

Lotus root is a symbolic ingredient during the Mid-Autumn Festival, representing abundance and harmony. Its circular slices with distinctive hole patterns symbolize family reunion and togetherness, making this dish perfect for gathering with loved ones under the harvest moon.
